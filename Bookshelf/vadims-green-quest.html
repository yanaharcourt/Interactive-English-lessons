Introduction

Meet Vadim! He's 9 years old and loves building with Lego, playing Clash of Clans and watching videos on YouTube. Vadim is an ordinary boy, but soon something extraordinary will happen that will change his view of the world around him.

Join his adventure!

One Saturday afternoon, Vadim was finishing his Lego castle when he heard a strange sound outside his window.

"Flap... flap... chirp!"

Looking out of the window, Vadim saw a small grey bird. Its wing was tangled in a plastic drinks holder.

"Oh no!" exclaimed Vadim and ran outside.

"It's all right, little one," he whispered. "I'll help you."

Carefully, Vadim freed the bird's wing. The bird chirped as if in thanks, flapped its wings and flew to a nearby tree.

Vadim looked at the plastic in his hand. "This could have really hurt you," he thought. Looking around, Vadim noticed that plastic rubbish was everywhere: in bushes, under trees, along the pavement.

It was at that moment that Vadim's ecological adventure began.

That evening, while playing Multicraft, Vadim couldn't stop thinking about the bird.

"What if I build something to help the environment, just like I do in Multicraft?" thought Vadim.

In a new Multicraft world, Vadim built a recycling centre. He created a sorting system for different materials, added water channels for cleaning and ovens for processing.

"This is brilliant," said Vadim, admiring his creation. "But what if I could do something like this in real life?"

At breakfast, Vadim asked his parents: "What happens to all our rubbish after we throw it away?"

His parents explained about dumps, recycling centres, and how some plastic ends up in oceans and forests where it can harm animals.

"I want to start recycling," declared Vadim. "Could we have separate bins for different rubbish?"

Together they set up three containers in the kitchen: one for paper, one for plastic, and one for food waste that could be composted.

Vadim made colourful signs for each bin, just like the signs in his Multicraft world. Now his digital creation was becoming reality.

A few days later, Vadim's class watched a video about water conservation at school. The video showed how many animals lose their habitats because of water pollution and waste.

Vadim immediately thought of the bird he had saved. "All animals need clean water," he whispered to himself.

That afternoon, Vadim became a "Water Guardian". He decided to track how much water his family used every day.

When he brushed his teeth in the morning, Vadim noticed that he usually left the tap running the whole time.

"That's wasting water!" he realised, quickly turning off the tap.

By the end of the day, Vadim was shocked. "Wow, we use so much water every day!"

Vadim created a "Water Saving Challenge" for his family with simple rules:

  1. Turn off the tap while brushing teeth
  2. Take shorter showers (use a timer!)
  3. Use a watering can for plants instead of a hose
  4. Collect rainwater in a bucket for the garden
  5. Report any dripping taps immediately!
