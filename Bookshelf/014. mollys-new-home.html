Focus Question

How do Molly's feelings about her new home change during the story? Why?

Words to Know

  • bakery
  • cheer
  • stale
  • sweet
  • swing
  • traffic

Molly sat in her new room.

She and her dad had just moved to the city.

Their old home was in the country.

Nothing was the same.

Her old home had a big yard.

It had tall trees and a tire swing.

Molly and her cousins would play for hours.

This home was always loud.

There was nothing but traffic outside.

Dad tried to cheer Molly up.

He got a cherry pie at the bakery down the street.

Molly loved cherry pie.

Her aunt used to bake one each week.

Her pie was warm and sweet.

This pie was cold and stale.

Molly pushed the plate away.

"I miss our home," she said.

Dad gave her a sad smile.

A few days later, a family came over.

They lived next door.

Their girl was the same age as Molly.

"I'm Nina. Would you like to come to the park with us?" she asked.

Molly didn't know the city had parks.

Dad said they could go.

There were no tall trees in the park.

There was a tire swing, though!

Molly and Nina played for hours.

Later, they shared Nina's favorite cookies.

They were warm and sweet.

Molly smiled.

Maybe the city is okay after all, she thought.

Words to Know

bakery (n.)
a place that makes and sells bread, cakes, and other baked food

cheer (v.)
to make someone happier

stale (adj.)
no longer fresh or delicious; old and dry

sweet (adj.)
having the taste of sugar

swing (n.)
a seat that hangs from ropes or chains and moves back and forth

traffic (n.)
the movement of vehicles on a road or highway
