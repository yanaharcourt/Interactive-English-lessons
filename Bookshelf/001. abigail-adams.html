Focus Question

Who was Abigail Adams, and why was she important?

Words to Know

  • colonies
  • First Lady
  • Great Britain
  • respected
  • vice president
  • war

Who Was Abigail Adams?

Abigail Adams was smart.

She had good ideas.

This painting of Abigail Adams was made in 1795.

Early Years

Abigail was born in 1744.

She did not go to school.

Her mom taught her to read and write.

Abigail married John Adams.

They lived on a farm.

Abigail ran the farm.

War with Great Britain

The United States was not a country at that time.

It was thirteen colonies.

They were ruled by Great Britain.

People in the colonies had to pay taxes to Great Britain. This stamp showed that a tax had been paid.

Many people did not want to be part of Great Britain.

John worked to help them break away.

John was away a lot.

This painting shows John Adams meeting with other men to talk about the colonies breaking away from Great Britain.

Abigail cared for the family.

She also ran the farm.

Ideas for a Better Country

Abigail and John wrote each other many letters.

She told him her ideas.

John respected her ideas.

Abigail wanted to end slavery.

She also wanted girls to go to school.

When they grew up, she wanted girls to have the right to vote.

All of these were good ideas before their time.

Abigail cared for and served meals to soldiers who were hurt during the war.

The colonies went to war with Great Britain.

The colonies won the war in 1781.

They became the United States.

After the War

John became the first vice president.

Then he became the second president.

John and Abigail were the first president and First Lady to live in the White House. It was called the President's House at the time.

This painting of Abigail Adams was made late in her life.

Abigail was a smart First Lady.

She told people her ideas.

Some people did not like that.

Abigail told them anyway.

Later Years

Abigail died in 1818.

Her ideas lived on.

Now, they make the United States a better place.

This statue of Abigail Adams is in Boston, Massachusetts.

Great Britain (n.)
an island with the countries of England, Scotland, and Wales

respected (v.)
treated with honor

vice president (n.)
a person second in charge of a country

war (n.)
