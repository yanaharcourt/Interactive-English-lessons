Focus Question

How can you make art from ice?

Words to Know

  • artists
  • carve
  • melt
  • shape
  • statue
  • tools

Introduction

Have you ever made ice cubes in the freezer?

Ice artists make giant blocks of ice.

Then they turn the blocks into works of art.

Ice Artists

Ice artists use big machines that make clear ice.

Sometimes they also use white ice.

Do You Know?

Some ice artists compete to make the tallest ice statue in the world.

In 2009, ice artists built a 48-foot (14 m) statue of Scrat, a character from the Ice Age movies.

An ice artist might add dyes to make ice different colors.

Some artists freeze flowers or other things in the ice.

Ice artists use sharp tools and even flames to shape the ice.

A chain saw can cut through hard ice quickly.

Making a Statue

It can take an hour to make a simple ice statue.

A big ice statue can take many hours or even days.

Ice statues in cold places can last for months before they melt.

Ice statues in warm places might only last for a few hours.

Moving a Statue

Ice artists sometimes drive their statues to parties. It can take a lot of work to move ice statues.

Artists wrap their statues in special blankets that keep the statues cold instead of warm. The artists also use dry ice to keep the statues from melting.

Amazing Ice Statues

Ice artists can make almost anything out of ice.

They can carve ice to look like giant bugs.

They can build a slide.

Ice artists can even make big buildings.

People can spend the night in some of them.

Even the beds are made of ice!

Would you want to spend the night in a room made of ice?

Conclusion

It takes hard work and sharp tools to turn ice into works of art.

Sooner or later, the statues melt away.

Then it's time to make more ice!

Words to Know

artists (n.)
people who are good at painting, music, writing, or another art or craft

carve (v.)
to cut with care; to make something, such as a sculpture, by carefully cutting and shaping a material

melt (v.)
to change from a solid to a liquid, mainly due to the temperature getting warmer

shape (v.)
to use hands or a tool to make something into a shape

statue (n.)
a three-dimensional work of art, usually in the shape of a person or other animal

tools (n.)
objects or devices that make a job or task easier
