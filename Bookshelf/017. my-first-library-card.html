Focus Question

What is a library card, and how do you use it?

Words to Know

  • borrow
  • checked out
  • due
  • favorite
  • library
  • scans

At the library, I've always checked out books with Mom's card.

Today I'm getting my own card, though.

Any kid can get one.

We walk up to the librarian at the counter.

I tell her my name, address, and phone number.

I tell her my birth date, too.

I promise to return everything I borrow.

If I lose something, we have to pay for it.

I promise to try to return things on time, too.

Then the librarian hands me a card.

I sign my name on the line.

Now the card is really mine.

The card is also free.

With it, I can borrow books, movies, and more!

Mom and I walk over to the books.

I take some time to pick out the ones I want to borrow today.

Some are favorite books I've read before.

Others are new to me.

I pick out my favorite movie, too.

Then we return to the counter.

I hand my card to the librarian.

She scans it.

She tells me that if I want to, I can also scan things myself.

Next, she scans my things and hands them back to me.

She tells me when they are due back.

Mom and I leave the library.

When we return these things, I want to check out even more!

Words to Know

borrow (v.)
to take something with the promise to return it later

checked out (v.)
borrowed something from a library for a certain period of time

due (adj.)
expected or required at a certain time

favorite (adj.)
liked more than all other similar things

library (n.)
a place where books, magazines, movies, and other materials are kept for borrowing

scans (v.)
passes an object over or through a beam of light to get information about it
