Bonjour

Good morning / Good day

When to use BONJOUR?

Bonjour is the most common greeting in French

Use it from morning until evening (roughly until 6 PM)

Usage

When and where

  • Time: Morning until evening
  • Context: Formal and informal
  • Who: Anyone (strangers, friends)
  • Where: Shops, streets, work
  • Tone: Polite and neutral

Pronunciation

How to say it

  • Bonjour = bon-ZHOOR
  • "Bon" = like "bone" without the 'e'
  • "jour" = like "zhoor" (soft 'j')
  • Stress on the second syllable
  • Don't pronounce the final 'r' strongly
Bonjour! ‚Üí Hello! / Good morning!
Bonjour, madame. ‚Üí Good morning, madam.
Bonjour, comment allez-vous? ‚Üí Hello, how are you?

Bonsoir

Good evening

When to use BONSOIR?

Bonsoir is used in the evening and at night

Use it from around 6 PM onwards

Usage

When and where

  • Time: Evening and night
  • Context: Formal and informal
  • Who: Anyone you meet
  • Where: Restaurants, parties, home
  • Note: Also used when leaving

Pronunciation

How to say it

  • Bonsoir = bon-SWAR
  • "Bon" = like "bone" without the 'e'
  • "soir" = like "swar"
  • Stress on the second syllable
  • Smoother than English "evening"

Salut

Hi / Bye (Informal)

When to use SALUT?

Salut is INFORMAL - only use with friends and family!

As "Hello"

Informal greeting

  • Time: Any time of day
  • Who: Friends, family, children
  • Tone: Casual and friendly
  • Similar to: "Hi" or "Hey"
  • Don't use: With strangers or bosses!

As "Goodbye"

Informal farewell

  • Salut can mean "Bye" too!
  • Very casual way to say goodbye
  • Use with same people as greeting
  • Pronunciation: sah-LOO
  • Don't pronounce the final 't'

Merci

Thank You

Essential word for being polite in French!

Ways to Say Thank You

Different levels

  • Merci - Thank you
  • Pronunciation: mehr-SEE
  • Merci beaucoup - Thank you very much
  • Merci bien - Thanks a lot
  • Merci infiniment - Thanks immensely

Responding to Merci

You're welcome

  • De rien - You're welcome (casual)
  • Literally: "Of nothing"
  • Je vous en prie - You're welcome (formal)
  • Il n'y a pas de quoi - Don't mention it
  • Avec plaisir - With pleasure
