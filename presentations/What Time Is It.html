⏰ What Time Is It?

British vs American English

Today we'll learn how to tell time in British and American English!

🇬🇧 British English

More formal expressions

quarter past, half past

Traditional time telling

🇺🇸 American English

More casual expressions

quarter after, thirty

Modern time telling

🌟 Main Differences:

3:15 → British: quarter past three | American: quarter after three
3:30 → British: half past three | American: three thirty
🕐
📝

📝 Basic Time Structure

How to read the clock

12
3:30

🇬🇧 British English

  • It's half past three
  • More formal structure
  • Uses "past" and "to"
  • Hour comes after minute

🇺🇸 American English

  • It's three thirty
  • More casual structure
  • Uses "after" and "before"
  • Hour comes first

⏰ Basic Pattern:

British: It's [minute] [past/to] [hour]
American: It's [hour] [minute/after/before]

🔄 Quarter Hours

15, 30, and 45 minutes

3:15
🇬🇧 Quarter past three
🇺🇸 Quarter after three
🇺🇸 Three fifteen
3:30
🇬🇧 Half past three
🇺🇸 Three thirty
🇺🇸 Half after three
3:45
🇬🇧 Quarter to four
🇺🇸 Quarter before four
🇺🇸 Three forty-five

📋 Key Differences:

British uses: past for after the hour, to for before the hour
American uses: after for after the hour, before for before the hour
American also says: the exact numbers "three fifteen"

🔢 Other Minutes

Non-quarter times

3:05
🇬🇧 Five past three
🇺🇸 Five after three
🇺🇸 Three oh five
3:20
🇬🇧 Twenty past three
🇺🇸 Twenty after three
🇺🇸 Three twenty
3:50
🇬🇧 Ten to four
🇺🇸 Ten before four
🇺🇸 Three fifty

🇬🇧 British Pattern

  • 0-30 minutes: [minutes] past [hour]
  • 31-59 minutes: [60-minutes] to [next hour]
  • Example: 3:40 = Twenty to four
  • More mathematical approach

🇺🇸 American Pattern

  • Simple: [hour] [minutes]
  • Alternative: [minutes] after/before [hour]
  • Example: 3:40 = Three forty
  • More direct approach

🎯 O'clock

Exact hours

Both British and American use "O'clock"

For exact hours only (no minutes)

3:00 = Three o'clock

3:00
✅ Three o'clock
12:00
✅ Twelve o'clock
🌅 Noon / Midday
🌙 Midnight
6:00
✅ Six o'clock

⚠️ Important Notes:

Only use "o'clock" when there are NO minutes
Wrong: Three fifteen o'clock
Right: Three o'clock or Three fifteen

🌅 AM and PM

Time of day

🌅 AM (Ante Meridiem)

  • Before noon
  • 12:00 AM = Midnight
  • 6:00 AM = Six in the morning
  • 11:59 AM = Just before noon

🌙 PM (Post Meridiem)

  • After noon
  • 12:00 PM = Noon
  • 6:00 PM = Six in the evening
  • 11:59 PM = Just before midnight
8:30 AM
🇬🇧 Half past eight in the morning
🇺🇸 Eight thirty AM
2:15 PM
🇬🇧 Quarter past two in the afternoon
🇺🇸 Two fifteen PM
9:45 PM
🇬🇧 Quarter to ten in the evening
🇺🇸 Nine forty-five PM

🕛 24-Hour Format

Military time

24-Hour Clock

More common in British English for official times

15:30 = Three thirty (PM)

🇬🇧 British Usage

  • Common in official contexts
    Часто в официальных контекстах
  • Train timetables
    Расписания поездов
  • TV schedules
    Телепрограммы
  • Military and medical
    Военные и медицинские учреждения
  • Read as: "Fifteen thirty"
    Читается как: "Фифтин сёти"

🇺🇸 American Usage

  • Less common in daily life
    Реже в повседневной жизни
  • Mainly military contexts
    Главным образом в военных контекстах
  • Called "military time"
    Называется "военное время"
  • Hospitals and emergency services
    Больницы и службы экстренного реагирования
  • Read as: "Fifteen hundred hours"
    Читается как: "Фифтин хандред ауэрс"

🇺🇸 Eighteen forty-five hours

🇺🇸 Zero zero thirty hours

💬 Common Expressions

Asking and telling time

🇬🇧 Asking Time (British)

  • What time is it?
  • Have you got the time?
  • Could you tell me the time?
  • What's the time?

🇺🇸 Asking Time (American)

  • What time is it?
  • Do you have the time?
  • Can you tell me what time it is?
  • What time do you have?

💬 Conversation Examples:

A: Excuse me, what time is it?
B (British): It's half past three.
B (American): It's three thirty.
A: When does the meeting start?
B (British): At quarter to nine.
B (American): At eight forty-five.
