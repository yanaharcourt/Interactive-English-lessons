Today we'll learn Adverbs of Frequency - words that tell us how often something happens!

💯 High Frequency

Always, Usually, Often

High Frequency Words

Actions that happen very often or all the time

80% - 100% of the time

📝 Very High Frequency

90-100%

  • Always - 100%
  • Usually - 90%
  • Generally - 90%
  • Normally - 90%

🎯 High Frequency

70-80%

  • Often - 70%
  • Frequently - 70%
  • Regularly - 70%
  • Commonly - 70%
I always brush my teeth. → I brush my teeth every day.
She usually walks to school. → She walks to school most days.
We often play football. → We play football many times.
🔄
⚖️

🔄 Medium Frequency

Sometimes, Occasionally

Medium Frequency Words

Actions that happen from time to time

20% - 80% of the time

📊 Medium-High

50-80%

  • Sometimes - 50%
  • Occasionally - 30%
  • From time to time - 40%

📉 Medium-Low

20-50%

  • Not often - 30%
  • Once in a while - 25%
  • Every now and then - 30%
I sometimes eat pizza. → I eat pizza some days, not every day.
She occasionally goes to the cinema. → She goes to the cinema not very often.
We sometimes have homework. → We have homework some days.

🚫

❌ Low Frequency

Rarely, Seldom, Never

Low Frequency Words

Actions that happen very rarely or never

0% - 20% of the time

📉 Low Frequency

  • Rarely - 10%
  • Seldom - 10%
  • Hardly ever - 5%

🚫 No Frequency

  • Never - 0%
  • Not at all - 0%
  • Under no circumstances - 0%

🎯 Position Rule:

Frequency adverbs usually come BEFORE the main verb
but AFTER the verb "to be"

📍
🔄

📍 Word Order

Where do we put frequency adverbs?

✅ Before Main Verb

  • I always study
  • She often runs
  • They sometimes cook

✅ After "to be"

  • I am always happy
  • She is usually busy
  • They are never late

✅ After Auxiliary

  • I have never seen
  • She has always lived
  • They will often visit

🌟 Examples with different sentence types:

Simple Present: I always eat breakfast.
With "to be": She is sometimes tired.
Present Perfect: We have never been there.
