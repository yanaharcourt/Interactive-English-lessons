What is a NOUN?

A noun names a person, place, thing, or idea

Definition

A NOUN is a word that names something

If you can point to it, see it, or think about it - it's probably a noun!

Types of Nouns

What nouns name

  • üë§ People: boy, teacher, Maria
  • üè´ Places: school, park, London
  • üìö Things: book, car, apple
  • üí≠ Ideas: love, freedom, happiness
  • üêï Animals: dog, cat, elephant

  • ‚úÖ Can you count it? ‚Üí NOUN
  • ‚úÖ Can you put "a" or "the" before it? ‚Üí NOUN
  • ‚úÖ Does it answer "What?" or "Who?" ‚Üí NOUN

Common & Proper Nouns

Two types of nouns

The Difference

Common nouns are general. Proper nouns are specific names.

Proper nouns ALWAYS start with a CAPITAL LETTER!

Common Nouns

General names (lowercase)

  • boy - any boy
  • city - any city
  • dog - any dog
  • school - any school
  • country - any country

Proper Nouns

Specific names (CAPITAL)

  • Tom - a specific boy
  • London - a specific city
  • Rex - a specific dog
  • Cambridge School - a specific school
  • England - a specific country
My friend lives in a city. ‚Üí common nouns
Anna lives in Paris. ‚Üí proper nouns (capitals!)
The teacher is called Mr. Smith. ‚Üí common + proper

Singular & Plural

One or Many?

The Rule

Singular = ONE thing. Plural = TWO or MORE things.

How to Make Plurals

Basic rules

  • Add -s: cat ‚Üí cats
  • Add -es: box ‚Üí boxes
  • -y ‚Üí -ies: baby ‚Üí babies
  • -f ‚Üí -ves: leaf ‚Üí leaves
  • Irregular: child ‚Üí children

Examples

Singular ‚Üí Plural

  • book ‚Üí books
  • dish ‚Üí dishes
  • city ‚Üí cities
  • knife ‚Üí knives
  • man ‚Üí men
  • foot ‚Üí feet
I have one book. ‚Üí singular
I have three books. ‚Üí plural (add -s)
The children are playing. ‚Üí irregular plural

What is a VERB?

Action words and state of being

Definition

A VERB is a word that shows action or state of being

Action Verbs

Things you DO

Physical or mental actions

run, jump, eat, think
write, play, sing, read

State of Being Verbs

How things ARE

Being, not doing

am, is, are, was, were
be, being, been

Easy Test for Verbs

  • ‚úÖ Can you DO it? ‚Üí Action Verb
  • ‚úÖ Does it show a state? ‚Üí Being Verb
  • ‚úÖ Can you add -ing? ‚Üí Verb (running, eating)
  • ‚úÖ Does it change with time? ‚Üí Verb (walk/walked)
The dog runs fast. ‚Üí action verb
She is happy. ‚Üí being verb
We play football. ‚Üí action verb

Action Verbs

Verbs that show what someone DOES

Action verbs tell us what is happening!

Physical Actions

Things you can see

  • run - He runs every day.
  • jump - The cat jumps high.
  • eat - We eat lunch at noon.
  • write - She writes a letter.
  • dance - They dance together.

Mental Actions

Things you can't see

  • think - I think about you.
  • know - She knows the answer.
  • believe - We believe in magic.
  • understand - He understands English.
  • remember - I remember that day.
The bird flies in the sky. ‚Üí physical action
Maria loves chocolate. ‚Üí mental action
They play games every weekend. ‚Üí physical action

Being Verbs

The verb "TO BE"

Being verbs don't show action - they show a STATE or CONDITION

Present Tense (Now)

Forms of "be"

  • I am - I am happy.
  • You are - You are tall.
  • He/She/It is - She is smart.
  • We are - We are friends.
  • They are - They are students.

Past Tense (Before)

Forms of "be"

  • I was - I was tired.
  • You were - You were late.
  • He/She/It was - He was sick.
  • We were - We were at home.
  • They were - They were happy.
I am a student. ‚Üí present being
She was at school yesterday. ‚Üí past being
They are my friends. ‚Üí present being

Making Sentences

NOUN + VERB = Complete Sentence!

Every sentence needs a NOUN (who/what) and a VERB (does/is)

üìù Sentence Formula

NOUN + VERB = SENTENCE

Dogs + bark = Dogs bark.
She + runs = She runs.
We + are = We are students.

Examples

  • Birds fly.
  • Maria reads books.
  • The cat is sleeping.
  • Children play games.
  • I love chocolate.
The dog barks loudly. ‚Üí NOUN + VERB
Students study every day. ‚Üí NOUN + VERB
