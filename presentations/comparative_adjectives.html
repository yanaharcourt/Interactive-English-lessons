üéØ Memory Tips

üìù Spelling Rules

Special spelling changes

üë• Double the consonant

One vowel + one consonant ‚Üí double the consonant + er

  • big ‚Üí bigger
  • fat ‚Üí fatter
  • hot ‚Üí hotter
  • sad ‚Üí sadder
  • thin ‚Üí thinner

üîÑ Change y to i

Consonant + y ‚Üí consonant + ier

  • happy ‚Üí happier
  • easy ‚Üí easier
  • lazy ‚Üí lazier
  • busy ‚Üí busier
  • funny ‚Üí funnier

‚ú® Drop silent e

Words ending in silent 'e' ‚Üí drop 'e' + er

  • nice ‚Üí nicer
  • large ‚Üí larger
  • simple ‚Üí simpler
  • brave ‚Üí braver
  • late ‚Üí later
  • wide ‚Üí wider

üîç How to know which rule to use?

1. Count syllables

1-2 syllables = usually add -er

3+ syllables = usually add "more"

2. Look at the ending

Consonant + y = change to -ier

Short vowel + consonant = double + er

3. Check if irregular

good, bad, far, little = special forms

When in doubt, check a dictionary!

üí° Practice tip:

Try saying both forms out loud:
"more happy" vs "happier" - which sounds better?
Your ear will often guide you to the right answer!
