⏰ Past Simple

Yesterday's Stories

Today we'll learn Past Simple - the tense we use to talk about things that happened yesterday, last week, or long ago!

🌟 Why do we need Past Simple?

To tell stories! To talk about what we did yesterday, last summer, or when we were little!
"I played football yesterday." 📅
"She visited her grandma last week." 👵

✅ Regular Verbs

Just add -ed!

Regular Verbs Formula

Present Verb + ED = Past Simple

Most verbs follow this easy rule!

🎯 Simple Addition

Just add -ed

  • walk → walked
  • play → played
  • watch → watched
  • listen → listened
  • help → helped

📋 Special Rules

Some need small changes

  • study → studied (y→i)
  • stop → stopped (double p)
  • like → liked (just +d)
  • dance → danced (just +d)
  • cry → cried (y→i)
Yesterday, I played video games. → I had fun playing yesterday!
She watched a movie last night. → The movie finished, she's not watching now.
We walked to school this morning. → The walk to school is finished.

🔄 Irregular Verbs

The shape-shifters!

Irregular Verbs are Special!

They don't follow the -ed rule. They change into completely different words!

Like magic! 🪄 GO becomes WENT!

GO WENT
EAT ATE
SEE SAW
COME CAME
TAKE TOOK
GIVE GAVE

💡 How to remember irregular verbs?

Practice, practice, practice!
Read stories, sing songs, play games!
The more you use them, the easier they become!

I went to the park yesterday. → GO becomes WENT in the past
She ate an apple for lunch. → EAT becomes ATE in the past
We saw a rainbow after the rain. → SEE becomes SAW in the past

❓ Questions & Negatives

Meet your helper: DID!

🦸‍♂️ DID is our superhero helper!

When we ask questions or say "no" in the past, we use DID!
And here's the magic: when DID comes, the main verb goes back to its present form!

DID + subject + base verb?
Subject + DID NOT + base verb

❓ Questions

  • Did you play football?
  • Did she go to school?
  • Did they eat dinner?
  • Did he watch TV?

Notice: PLAY, GO, EAT, WATCH (base form!)

🚫 Negatives

  • I didn't play football
  • She didn't go to school
  • They didn't eat dinner
  • He didn't watch TV

didn't = did not (short form)

🎯 Remember the rule:

Positive: I played, She went
Question: Did you play? Did she go?
Negative: I didn't play, She didn't go

Question: Did you see the movie? → Asking about the past
Negative: I didn't see the movie. → Saying "no" about the past
Positive: Yes, I saw it! → Confirming what happened

📅 Time Words

When did it happen?

Past Time Words

These words tell us it happened in the past!

They're like signals that say: "Use Past Simple here!"

🕐 Yesterday Times

  • Yesterday
  • Yesterday morning
  • Yesterday evening
  • The day before yesterday

📆 Last Times

  • Last week
  • Last month
  • Last year
  • Last summer

🕰️ Ago & When

  • Two days ago
  • A week ago
  • When I was young
  • In 2020
Yesterday I played with my friends.
Last week we went to the zoo.
I saw that movie two months ago.
When I was little, I loved ice cream.

🎯 Pro tip:

When you see these time words, think:
"This story is about the past! I need Past Simple!"
